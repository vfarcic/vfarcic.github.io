Note:
First problem: the agents are barking orders at the cloud - create this, change that, delete the other thing - imperative commands, executed the instant they're issued. So we flip it. We put a control plane in the middle, and instead of issuing commands, the agents declare the state they want. This is Crossplane. And here's the real win: your platform team defines what a "database" even is - and, just as importantly, how it complies with company rules. Encryption on, backups configured, only the approved regions, the right tags for cost - all baked into that one blessed abstraction. So instead of the agent picking the right two hundred knobs out of thousands and hoping it's compliant, it just asks for a database, and what it gets is compliant by construction. The agent can only ask for what your API exposes, so the blast radius shrank to exactly what you allow. But notice: the agents are still applying straight to the control plane. It goes live the instant they say so. No review, no history, no undo.

# Infrastructure with AI Agents for Dummies

AI agents are amplifiers. Give one to an experienced engineer and great things happen faster. Give one to someone who doesn't know what they're doing and you get a disaster at a scale, and speed, that was previously unimaginable. This session is a live demonstration of both.

An AI agent, a cloud account, and a one-line prompt asking for a database. A few minutes later, the database is up and running. Success? Not quite. Nothing visibly failed, and that's exactly the problem. "It works" hides everything that's wrong underneath, and what's wrong underneath is a lot.

The fix is not a better prompt. It's better foundations. The same one-line request, repeated over progressively stronger foundations, with every iteration removing another way to cause damage and replacing questions nobody should be forced to answer with a few that anyone can. The destination: a setup where an agent, or a human, is fully productive yet unable to do much harm, even when they try.

Along the way: what agents are genuinely great at, where they will confidently lead you off a cliff, and the one thing they will never do for you, which is knowing what you're doing. AI didn't remove the need for expertise. It amplified it.

## Benefits to CNCF Ecosystem

This session demonstrates how CNCF projects form the guardrails that make AI-driven infrastructure management safe. Kubernetes as a control plane, Crossplane for declarative resource management and Compositions, and Argo CD for GitOps are shown not as individual tools but as layers of a single architecture that turns unpredictable AI agents into reliable platform consumers.

The talk addresses one of the most pressing questions in the ecosystem today: how to adopt AI agents for operations without sacrificing the declarative, reconciled, auditable foundations the cloud-native community spent a decade building? The answer presented here is that those foundations are precisely what makes agent adoption safe, which gives the audience a concrete, demonstrable reason to deepen their investment in CNCF technologies.

## Key takeaways:

* Why AI agents amplify both expertise and incompetence, demonstrated live with real cloud resources
* Why "it works" is the most dangerous outcome of agent-driven infrastructure
* Which tools agents should never use to manage resources, and what to give them instead
* How to narrow the interface so agents and humans only answer questions they're qualified to answer
* How to remove direct access to everything while keeping everyone fully productive
* How to use agents as teachers rather than as an excuse for not knowing things
